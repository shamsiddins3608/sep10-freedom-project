# Blog 7: Presentation Reflection

## Introduction

The final part of the Freedom Project was presenting my website both in class and at the SEP Expo. I learned about construction technology and demonstrate the website that I spent hours creating. Although I was anxious during the presentations, they helped me improve my communication skills and reflect on how i can speak better.

## Takeaways from My In-Class Presentation

During my in-class presentation, I used all of my slides and explained all of my project. One thing I noticed was that I was very anxious because everyone was staring at me, even though I had a microphone i was still speaking quietly at times. I also realized that my tone of voice did not change much throughout the presentation, which made my delivery less engaging than I wanted it to be. I didnt have much eye contact because i was focusing on the text, slides, and my website. Another thing I noticed afterward was that I forgot to demonstrate the responsiveness of my website.

Even with these challenges, I think that the presentation went well. I was able to explain my topic, demonstrate how technology is used in construction, and show the A-Frame model that I worked hard to create.

## Takeaways from My Expo Elevator Pitch

The Expo elevator pitch was much different from the classroom presentation. Because it was short, I only used two slides from my presentation. At first, I felt rushed and was trying to fit a lot of information into a small amount of time.

However, the more I practiced my elevator pitch, the better it became. Each time I presented, I became more comfortable explaining my project and focusing on the most important details. Instead of talking about every part of my website, I learned how to summarize my project quickly and clearly.

The Expo taught me that being able to communicate ideas efficiently is an important skill. It also helped me become more confident when talking about my project with different people.

## Engineering Design Process

At this stage of the **engineering design process**, I have completed the testing, evaluation, and communication stages of my project. Presenting my website allowed me to evaluate what worked well and what could be improved. Through reflection, I identified areas where I can improve future presentations, such as speaking louder, varying my tone, and remembering to demonstrate all of my website's features.

## Skills

Throughout the presentation process, I developed several important **skills**.

### Communication Skills

I improved my ability to explain my project to different audiences. The elevator pitch especially helped me learn how to communicate important information in a short amount of time.

### Public Speaking Skills

Although I was anxious, presenting in front of my classmates and Expo judges. The presenting helped me become more comfortable speaking publicly. I learned that preparation and practice can make presentations feel easier.

### Reflection Skills

Reflecting on my presentations helped me recognize both my strengths and weaknesses. Understanding what I did well and what I can improve will help me in future projects and presentations.

## What I Am Proud Of

One thing I am most proud of is how my website and presentation slides turned out. Throughout the year, my coding level has improved significantly. My websites became more organized and interactive over time, especially with my A-Frame model. I am also proud that my slides are colorful, and creative. Looking back at my old websites/projects, I can clearly see how much progress I made, and improved.

## Conclusion

Overall, both presentations were valuable learning experiences. The in-class presentation helped me learn where I can improve my public speaking, while the Expo elevator pitch taught me how to communicate my ideas more efficiently. Even though I was nervous, I am proud of the website and presentation that I created. The Freedom Project helped me learn more about construction technology, web development, A-Frame, and presentation skills, and I am happy with how much I improved throughout the process.
